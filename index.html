Hi! I'm Vera.

I make websites for small businesses and non-profit organizations. I work with customizable CMS systems like WordPress, frameworks like Bootstrap, or my own brain and hand-coded CSS and Javascript (like this site!) to make pages look, react, and respond exactly the way you and I both want them to.

Before I started developing, I was a special education teacher in DC — and before that, I was an English teacher in Japan — and before THAT, I worked as a freelance production assistant for New York-based indie film and television companies. I have a lot of eclectic life experience to bring to the table; I can also adapt to just about anything that comes my way. Responsivity and accessibility are always on my mind, not just for web development, but for everything in life. Check out some of my work below!

Like what you see? Write me at svsticker at gmail dot com or find me on: Github LinkedIn

Ganymede Technology

Icon, logo, and single-page website designed from scratch for a local IT business looking to enhance its web presence. Fully mobile responsive, accessible, and written and tested to display consistently across all browsers.

Development info: Bootstrap 3, PHP

Take Back the Flag

Activist/political website designed, deployed, and maintained in custom Wordpress. Includes calendar and gallery widgets, a file upload form, and an ongoing blog, all custom-tailored. Fully responsive, consistent across all browsers, and accessibility-ready.

Development info: Wordpress.org, custom CSS3

Hard Lens Media

Simple, easy-to-use activist/political media blog designed and deployed in lightly customized Wordpress. Includes blog page, static pages, and integrated Facebook and Twitter widgets. Fully responsive, consistent across all browsers, and accessibility-ready.

Development info: Wordpress.com, custom CSS3

Quote Generator

A basic random quote generator widget. Buttons allow the user to pull a random quote and share it using their Twitter account.

Development info: CSS3, JQuery

Your Weather Goes Here

A weather widget that checks if location information is allowed, and if so, fetches the temperature in Fahrenheit and Celsius. Background color changes depending on temperature.

Development info: CSS3, JQuery

Wikipedia Search

A search widget that searches for a word or phrase in Wikipedia and comes up with a list of articles, using custom animation, or allows user to search for a random article on the Wikipedia site.

Development info: Bootstrap 3, JQuery

Unpresidented

A Chrome/Firefox extension for political humor: takes all instances of "Donald Trump," "Donald J. Trump," etc., and replaces them with "Donny Trump;" takes all instances of "Steve Bannon" and replaces them with "President Bannon."

Development info: Javascript
